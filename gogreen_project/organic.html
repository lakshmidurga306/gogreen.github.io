Organic farming is an eco-friendly approach to agriculture that avoids synthetic chemicals and promotes biodiversity, sustainability, and soil health using natural processes. It works in harmony with nature rather than against it.

  • Maintain soil health using compost and crop rotation
  • Encourage biodiversity and eco-balance
  • Natural pest control with bio-solutions
  • Ensure animal welfare and organic feed
  • Focus on long-term sustainability
  • Preserve natural resources and ecosystems

  • Healthier, chemical-free food
  • Protects environment and biodiversity

  • Boosts rural employment
  • High market demand and value
  • Improves soil fertility long-term

Environmental and Ecological Protection

  • Waste Reduction: Organic farming minimizes waste through composting and recycling
  • Soil Health Improvement: Increases organic matter and microbial activity
  • Reduction of Chemical Use: Eliminates synthetic pesticides and fertilizers
  • Biodiversity Promotion: Supports diverse plant and animal life
  • Carbon Sequestration: Organic soils capture and store more carbon
  • Water Conservation: Reduces water pollution and improves retention

Types of Organic Farming

  • Pure Organic Farming Avoids all synthetic chemicals, using only natural inputs
  • Integrated Organic Farming Combines organic with modern sustainable techniques
  • Permaculture Mimics natural ecosystems for self-sustaining design
  • Biodynamic Farming Uses astrological and holistic practices with organic methods
  • Urban Organic Farming Organic farming in cities (rooftops, balconies, community gardens)
  • Aquaponics/Hydroponics Soil-less organic cultivation with water efficiency
